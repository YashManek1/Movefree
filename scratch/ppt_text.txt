Visually impaired individuals face significant challenges in navigating through unfamiliar environments safely and independently. Current solutions, such as guide dogs and cane systems, have limitations in detecting obstacles, providing real-time navigation, or offering dynamic interaction with the surrounding environment.
Additionally, there is a lack of effective communication between the visually impaired person and their family or caregivers regarding their safety and location.
This results in a need for a comprehensive, affordable, and portable navigation system that can help visually impaired individuals move safely, while also providing real-time updates to their family members.
How Are We Solving This:
MoveFree addresses the challenge of safe, independent navigation for visually impaired individuals in dynamic and unstructured environments. 
It provides real-time obstacle detection using ultrasonic sensors and an AI-powered vision system delivering timely voice alerts to avoid collisions. 
Additionally, the system supports voice-command control, point-to-point GPS navigation, and live video &  location sharing with family members for enhanced monitoring and support. 
This empowers users to move with confidence in public spaces, reducing dependence on others. 

MoveFree is an AI-powered wearable designed to enhance mobility and independence for visually impaired individuals. It integrates advanced technologies such as:

Real-Time Obstacle Detection
AI-Based Object & Hazard Recognition
Indoor & Outdoor Voice-Based Navigation 
Live Location Sharing & POV Video Streaming
Hands-Free Interaction via Voice Commands

By combining multi-modal sensing MoveFree empowers visually impaired individuals to move confidently and safely in complex environments.

Benchmarking Results 
1. Accuracy (mAP)
mAP@0.5 = 81% → Model detects objects correctly most of the time.
mAP@0.5:0.95 = 55% → Model is less precise when stricter overlap is required.
2. Interpretation
The model is very good at finding objects (high recall at IoU=0.5).
Bounding box placement is moderately precise (lower score at IoU=0.95).

2. Interpretation
The model is highly effective for real-time indoor guidance, specifically for safety-critical classes like stairs and people.
